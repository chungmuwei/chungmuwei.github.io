Handshake Theorem:

  • The sum of all vertex degrees is twice the number of edges.
  • It implies that the sum of all vertex degrees must be even.

Complete Graph: A graph with an edge between any pair of vertices, in other words, every vertex is adjacent to all the other vertices

Path:

A path in G from x to y is an alternating sequence of vertices and edges

Connected graph: For any two vertices x and y, there is a path from x to y

Disconnected graph: There exists two vertices x and y such that no path from x to y

Eulerian Circuit:

  • An undirected graph has an Eulerian cycle if and only if every vertex has even degree, and all of its vertices with nonzero degree belong to a single connected component.
